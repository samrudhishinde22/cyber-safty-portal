🔐 Cybersecurity Awareness & Protection

🌐 Stay Smart. Stay Safe. Stay Secure.

In today’s digital world, every click, login, and transaction carries a level of risk. Cybercriminals are constantly developing new ways to steal data, hack accounts, and commit fraud. Cybersecurity awareness is not just for experts — it is essential for every internet user. Understanding how threats work and how to avoid them is the first step toward protecting your digital life.

⚠️ Understanding Cyber Threats

Cyber threats are malicious activities designed to damage, steal, or gain unauthorized access to your personal or financial information. One of the most common threats is phishing, where attackers send fake emails, messages, or links that appear genuine to trick users into revealing sensitive information.

Similarly, malware is harmful software that can infect your device, steal data, or even lock your files through ransomware attacks. Hackers may also attempt identity theft and social engineering attacks to manipulate users into sharing confidential data.

🔑 Protecting Your Digital Identity

Your digital identity includes your passwords, personal information, bank details, and online accounts. Always create strong and unique passwords using letters, numbers, and special characters.

Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA) wherever possible. Never share your passwords, OTPs, or confidential information with anyone.

🌍 Safe Internet Practices

Always check for secure websites that use “https://” before entering sensitive information. Avoid clicking on unknown links or suspicious advertisements.

Do not use public Wi-Fi for sensitive activities like banking unless using a secure VPN connection.

📧 Email & Social Media Awareness

Be cautious when receiving unexpected emails asking for urgent action or personal details. Always verify the sender before clicking links.

Avoid sharing personal information like phone number, address, or travel plans on social media.

📱 Mobile & Device Security

Keep your device updated with the latest security patches. Install apps only from official app stores.

Use screen locks, PINs, or biometric security. Avoid connecting to unknown devices or public USB ports.

💳 Online Banking & Payment Safety

Never share your OTP, PIN, CVV, or banking passwords. Banks never ask for such details.

Always use official banking apps and check your statements regularly for suspicious activity.

🚨 What To Do If You Are a Victim

If you suspect a cyber attack, change your passwords immediately and inform your bank.

Report the incident on the official Indian Cyber Crime Portal: cybercrime.gov.in

🛡️ Final Safety Reminder

👉 Think before you click

👉 Verify before you trust

👉 Protect before you share

💡 Stay Alert – Your Safety Starts With You

A single careless click can lead to serious consequences. With the right awareness and habits, you can stay one step ahead of cybercriminals.
